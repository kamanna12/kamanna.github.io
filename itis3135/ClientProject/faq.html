FAQ's

What is lung cancer screening?

Lung cancer screening involves testing individuals who are at high risk of developing lung cancer but do not have any symptoms. It aims to detect lung cancer at an early stage when it is more treatable.

Who should undergo lung cancer screening?

Lung cancer screening is recommended for individuals who are between 55 and 80 years old, have a history of heavy smoking, and are current smokers or have quit within the last 15 years.

What are the benefits of lung cancer screening?

The main benefit of lung cancer screening is early detection, which can lead to better treatment outcomes and increased survival rates. It can also help detect other lung conditions early.

Are there any risks associated with lung cancer screening?

While lung cancer screening can help detect cancer early, it may also lead to false-positive results, unnecessary follow-up tests, and overdiagnosis. It's essential to discuss the benefits and risks with your healthcare provider.
